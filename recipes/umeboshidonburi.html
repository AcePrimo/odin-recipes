There's not a lot to this but there's elegance in simplicity, and this is elegant as FUCK.

The tools of the trade

  • a rice cooker
  • a mortar and pestle
  • a small bowl
  • a rice bowl

The ingredients for success

  • 1 cup rice uncooked
  • 2 umeboshi plums
  • salt to taste

Putting it all together

  1. Wash your rice (seriously, wash it)
  2. Put your washed rice into your rice cooker with the appropriate amount of water (rice cooker should indicate what amount of water is needed)
  3. Whilst your rice is cooking, pit your umeboshi and using your mortar and pestle, make a paste of the umeboshi, set paste into a small bowl
  4. When your rice has cooked, fluff it up, and put into a bowl
  5. Add umeboshi paste to the rice, ensuring rice is as evenly coated as you can get it

So that is it. Not a lot to it, but it's different and crazy refreshing. It goes nicely with pretty much any cut of meat you'd want to serve on top of rice. You can serve it with sashimi, or vegetables, or whatever you want. I'm a fan of eating it on it's own, and I'm hoping you like it too!
